About MimicDB

Integrins are heterodimeric receptors crucial for the differentiation, migration, adhesion and survival of metazoan cells. They mediate cell attachment via interactions with adhesion molecules and are also targeted by viper venom disintegrins and several viruses as entry points. The importance of integrin signaling is evident from integrin-linked diseases, ranging from developmental defects through Crohn's disease to tumor formation.

Despite their importance, for most of the 24 human integrins nothing is known about the molecular details of their interactions. This hampers their effective biomedical modulation and the successful targeting of integrin-related diseases. The moderate success of current targeting efforts have demonstrated the need for detailed structural understanding of various binding events. Thus, the delineation of the structural principles of known integrin interactions and the identification of unknown integrin binding modes in molecular detail is essential to understand the complex integrin signaling pathway.

MimicDB serves as a platform to enable such large-scale systematic research efforts by integrating available data on the sequences, subunits, structures, and expression patterns of all 24 human integrins, together with information about known extracellular integrin-binding ligands, and developed drugs that modulate these binding events.
